Higher Order Quantum Capacitance
& The Heptron Architecture

Author Kiprop Yego — Independent Researcher
Date March 2, 2026
Subject quant-ph · cond-mat.mes-hall
Status ● Pre-print / arXiv Submission
↓ Download PDF arXiv Submission → Cite / Collaborate →

Abstract

We present a comprehensive theoretical framework and device blueprint for a parafermionic Z₇ qudit — a seven-dimensional quantum information unit hosted in a topological quantum material engineered to support Z₇ clock-algebra zero modes. Starting from first principles of quantum materials science, we derive the higher-order quantum capacitance C⁽⁷⁾_Q of a Z₇ parafermionic island and show that it encodes a sevenfold degenerate ground state whose parity sectors are spectrally resolved through gate-voltage oscillations of period e/7. We develop a complete measurement-only topological quantum computing (MOTQC) architecture for the k = 7 case, generalising the Z₂ tetron device of Microsoft to a heptron: a hexagonal superconducting-topological hybrid island with six Z₇ parafermionic zero modes at its boundary. The full qudit Clifford group for d = 7 is generated by sequences of projective quantum-capacitance measurements, and we prove universality (up to magic-state injection) within this framework.

The Central Formula

The paper's primary theoretical contribution is the derivation of the Higher-Order Quantum Capacitance for a Z_k parafermionic zero-mode pair. For a general k:

Theorem 3.1
C⁽ᵏ⁾_Q |_{μ=0} = e²(k²−1) / 12k²k_BT
Quantum capacitance of a Z_k parafermionic zero-mode pair at the charge degeneracy point (μ = 0)

Specialising to k = 7 (the heptron case):

Equation 25
C⁽⁷⁾_Q |_{μ=0} = 4e² / 49k_BT
The higher-order quantum capacitance of the Z₇ parafermionic island at degeneracy. Compare: C⁽²⁾_Q = e²/16k_BT for Majorana (k=2).

This quantity — measurable via microwave reflectometry — exhibits six peaks per gate-voltage period e, with each peak encoding a distinct Z₇ charge sector m ∈ {0,…,6}. The sevenfold degeneracy is the operational primitive of the entire MOTQC architecture.

The Heptron Device

The heptron is a hexagonal superconducting island with six Z₇ parafermionic zero modes at its boundary — a direct generalisation of Microsoft's tetron (Z₂, 4 Majorana modes) to k = 7 (6 parafermionic modes). The hexagonal geometry is chosen because it supports six boundary modes under C₆ rotational symmetry, forming three commuting pairs that each span an independent Z₇ register.

The total zero-mode Hilbert space of the heptron is:

Equation — Hilbert Space
H₀ = C⁷ ⊗ C⁷ ⊗ C⁷ ≅ C³⁴³
343-dimensional zero-mode Hilbert space per heptron device, with three independent Z₇ qudit registers.

All logical operations — including the full d=7 Clifford group, the quantum Fourier transform F₇, and the two-qudit SUM gate — are implemented entirely through sequences of projective C⁽⁷⁾_Q measurements. No physical qubit rotations are required. This is the measurement-only paradigm, inherited and extended from the tetron architecture.

Materials Platforms

Realising Z₇ parafermionic zero modes requires engineering the ν = 1/7 fractional quantum Hall state proximitised by a superconductor. The paper identifies four candidate materials platforms:

GaAs/AlGaAs 2DEG — The ν = 1/7 Laughlin state has been observed in ultrahigh-mobility GaAs heterostructures. Proximity coupling to Nb or NbN is the most technically mature route, operating below 100 mK.

Graphene on hBN — The second Landau level of graphene on hexagonal boron nitride can host ν = 1/7 states at higher temperatures (100–300 mK) with Pb or MoRe superconductors.

Fractional Topological Insulator edge — Theoretically the most promising platform: a 3D topological insulator surface doped to ν = 1/7, whose edge modes mimic a FQH edge while potentially accessible above 1 K — dramatically relaxing dilution refrigerator requirements.

Key engineering targets derived in the paper: topological gap Δ_topo > 50 μeV, charging energy E_C > 500 μeV, quasiparticle poisoning rate Γ_QP < 1/τ_gate.
