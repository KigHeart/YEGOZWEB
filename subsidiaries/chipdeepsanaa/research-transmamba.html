TransMamba-MoE: A Sequence-Level Hybrid
Transformer–Mamba Architecture with Fine-Grained Mixture of Experts

Mathematical Foundations, Notation Derivation, and Resolution of Three Core Design Problems

Author Kiprop Yego — Independent Research
Date March 21, 2026
Subject cs.LG · cs.AI · cs.CL
Status ● Working Draft — Pre-Implementation
↓ Download PDF Also: Heptron Paper → Collaborate →

Abstract

TransMamba-MoE extends the TransMamba architecture [Li et al., AAAI 2026] — which partitions sequences between Transformer attention and Mamba SSM at a scheduled TransPoint — by incorporating a DeepSeekMoE-style fine-grained Mixture of Experts at three distinct sites within each layer: (i) the shared weight matrix pool (W-MoE), (ii) the SSM projection layers for Δ, A, and Z (SSM-MoE), and (iii) the feed-forward sublayer (FFN-MoE). We derive all notation from first principles, state and solve three core design problems at the intersection of MoE and TransMamba, and provide a complete implementation contract traceable to numbered equations and theorems.

The TransMamba-MoE Architecture

TransMamba routes each layer's token sequence through two mechanisms split at a scheduled TransPoint P_ℓ: tokens before P_ℓ go through Transformer attention; tokens from P_ℓ onward go through a Mamba SSM recurrence. Both mechanisms share a single weight matrix W via the algebraic duality Q↔C, K↔B, V↔x.

TransMamba-MoE replaces this single W with a pool of expert matrices and introduces MoE at three sites:

Three Design Problems Solved

The paper identifies and solves three fundamental design problems that arise when combining MoE routing with the TransMamba architecture:

Design Problem 1
The Expert-Conditioned Memory Converter

The TransMamba Memory Converter initialises the SSM hidden state from the attention segment's K and V matrices. When different tokens use different expert weight matrices W*_t, which value of W* is valid for the initialisation? Inconsistent expert selection breaks the algebraic duality that TransMamba relies upon.

✓ Solution — Definition 7.2 + Theorem 7.3

Derive the Expert-Conditioned MCE: h⁰ₛₛₘ = base term + Σ correction terms Φ_t. Proven error bound ‖h*₀ − h^MCE₀‖_F ≤ C_δ Σ ‖R_t‖²_F ‖h^s_t‖². Requires zero additional learned parameters.

Design Problem 2
Mechanism-Aware Load Balancing

Standard MoE applies a single global load-balancing loss across all tokens. But in TransMamba-MoE, attention-segment tokens and SSM-segment tokens have structurally different representations — so a single global coefficient will either over-balance (suppressing natural specialisation) or under-balance (allowing expert collapse in one segment).

✓ Solution — Theorem 8.4

Segment-specific losses L^(a)_bal and L^(s)_bal with coefficients αₐ = α₀/ρ_ℓ, αₛ = α₀ρ_ℓ where ρ_ℓ = P_ℓ/(T−P_ℓ). Proven strictly superior to any global coefficient. Equal total gradient magnitude from each segment regardless of TransPoint.

Design Problem 3
Training Stability under Coupled Instability

TransMamba already has a fragile training regime (Table 6 of Li et al. 2026). MoE routing introduces expert collapse as a second instability. In TransMamba-MoE these are coupled: TransPoint changes shift the routing distribution, which can trigger expert collapse in the newly dominant segment.

✓ Solution — Three-Component Protocol (Theorem 9.4)

(1) Gradual TransPoint warm-up schedule. (2) Decaying router noise ε_t ~ N(0,σ²_noise) for collapse prevention. (3) Segment-decoupled gradient clipping. Proven: E[‖∇_ϕ L_total‖²] → η²_clip as r → ∞.

The complete training objective combining all MoE balancing components:

L_total = L_CE + λₐ L^(a)_bal + λₛ L^(s)_bal + λ_sp L^(ssm-proj)_bal + λ_ffn L^(ffn)_bal

Implementation Contract

The paper provides a complete, unambiguous implementation contract in Section 12. Every data structure, invariant, and hyperparameter is specified so the coding phase is a transcription of mathematics, not ad-hoc engineering.

Key invariants: The Coupling Invariant (C1) — the routing decision E_t used for attention projections Q, K, V must be identical to that used for SSM projections C, B, x for the same token. Violation breaks the MCE derivation. The Load Balancing Accumulation (C5) — segment-specific load statistics must be accumulated across the full batch before computing the loss, not per-token-averaged.

Suggested first-run hyperparameters: 32 total experts (Ne=8, m=4), Ns=2 shared W-MoE experts, K=4 active routed, α₀=1×10⁻², R=2,000 warm-up steps, TransPoint schedule {0,128,256,512,1024,2048,4096,8192} cycled every 8 layers.
